Dimebag Darrell

The Guitar God

Darrell Lance Abbot A.K.A. "Dimebag Darrell" or "Dime" was an American guitarist for the bands Pantera and Damageplan.

A summary of Dime's life from 1966 - 2004 follows below:

  • 1966: Born in Ennis, Texas, U.S.A.

  • 1983: Dime and his brother Vincent Paul Abbott A.K.A. "Vinnie Paul" co-create the band Pantera and releases debut album "Metal Magic".

  • 1984: Pantera releases "Projects in the Jungle".

  • 1985: Pantera releases "I Am the Night".

  • 1988: Pantera releases "Power Metal".

  • 1990: Pantera releases "Cowboys from Hell".

  • 1992: Pantera releases "Vulgar Display of Power".

  • 1994: Pantera releases "Far Beyond Driven".

  • 1996: Pantera releases "The Great Southern Trendkill".

  • 2000: Pantera releases "Reinventing the Steel".

  • 2003: Pantera splits up due to problems with band members. Dime and Vinnie Paul form a new band named Damageplan.

  • 2004: Damageplan releases "New Found Power".

  • 2004: Dime is murdered in Columbus, Ohio, U.S.A.

Dime's legacy and influence is massively evident in modern metal music and aspiring guitarists worldwide. His signature heavy style along with high pitched "Dime-bomb" harmonics are still appreciated and imitated to this day.
